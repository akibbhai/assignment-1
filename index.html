"Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a visionary economist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, served as the Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh in 2007 during a transitional government period. Known globally as the "Father of Microfinance," Yunus revolutionized poverty alleviation through his Grameen Bank, empowering millions with small, collateral-free loans. His leadership extended beyond economics, advocating for social business and sustainable development. Dr. Yunus's profound impact continues to inspire efforts toward equity, inclusion, and a poverty-free world."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
